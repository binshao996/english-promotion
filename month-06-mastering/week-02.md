## 1. Grammar — 中国学习者 52 大常见错误

### 错误系统的结构

每一条错误都按以下格式展开：

```
❌ 常见错误 → ✅ 正确表达 → 🧠 原因：为什么中国学习者会犯这个错 → 🔧 如何纠正 → ✏️ 练习
```

你可能发现其中很多错误你也会犯——这正是本周的目的。发现并纠正一个错误，比你背 10 个新语法点更有价值。

这些错误不是"低级错误"；它们源于中英文之间根本的结构差异和你已经内化了的母语习惯。正视这些差异，系统归因，逐个打破——这就是从"看得懂但写不对"到"写得出且写得对"的关键一步。

### 1.1 冠词错误 (Article Errors) — 8 mistakes

中国学习者冠词错误率极高。根本原因不是不认真——而是**中文没有冠词系统**。中文通过语序和上下文表达"有定/无定"的区分（"一本书"vs"这本书"中的"一本"和"这"不是冠词），所以英语冠词是一个"从零开始建构"的认知系统。下面的每个错误都源于一个特定的中英认知差异。

---

**Error 1 — 可数名词单数裸奔 (Missing Article Before Singular Countable Noun)**

❌ I have **question** about the project.
✅ I have **a question** about the project.

🧠 中文："我有一个问题"在口语中可以说"我有问题"，"一个"不是必须的。所以很多中国学习者把"question"当作可以直接使用的词，忘了英语中单数可数名词必须有冠词/限定词。

🔧 在脑中建立检查点：每当你写一个单数可数名词（question, issue, problem, suggestion, idea...），立刻问自己——"前面有 a/an/the 吗？有 my/this/each 吗？"

✏️ 在以下句子中插入冠词（a/an/the）：
1. I need ___ clarification about this requirement.
2. She sent ___ email with ___ attachment.
3. There is ___ bug in ___ production environment.

---

**Error 2 — 抽象名词泛指加 the (The + Abstract Noun for General Reference)**

❌ **The knowledge** is power.
✅ **Knowledge** is power.

🧠 中文："知识就是力量"——中文没有冠词，学习者过度补偿，认为英语重要概念前应该加 the。规则是：抽象名词（knowledge, wisdom, love, technology, society...）泛指整个概念时用零冠词 ∅。只有当后面有限定修饰时（the knowledge **of physics**, the technology **behind AI**）才用 the。

🔧 训练自己的直觉：把 "the knowledge" 翻译成中文——"那个知识"——如果听起来奇怪，那就是泛指，不应该加 the。

✏️ 判断是否需要 the：
1. (∅ / The) Artificial intelligence is transforming industries.
2. (∅ / The) artificial intelligence that powers ChatGPT is based on transformers.
3. (∅ / The) happiness comes from meaningful work.

---

**Error 3 — 可数/不可数混淆导致的冠词错误 (Countable/Uncountable Confusion)**

❌ I need **an advice** from you. / Can you give me **a feedback**?
✅ I need **some advice** from you. / Can you give me **some feedback**? / I need **a piece of advice**.

🧠 中文："一个建议"、"一条反馈"——中文有量词（个、条），所有名词都可以用量词来"计数"。学习者自然地将"一个"映射为 a/an。但英语中 advice, feedback, information, furniture, equipment, knowledge, research, evidence 都是不可数名词，不能直接用 a/an。

🔧 记一张"不可数名词清单"贴在桌面上。每十个词一组，反复写直到习惯。最常见的不可数名词：advice, information, feedback, equipment, furniture, luggage, knowledge, research, evidence, progress, news, work, software, hardware, data（data 在学术写作中可用复数但日常中常作不可数）。

✏️ 改错：
1. I have an important information to share with the team.
2. We bought a new furniture for the office.
3. She gave me a very useful feedback.

---

**Error 4 — 最高级/序数词前遗漏 the (Missing the Before Superlative/Ordinal)**

❌ This is **best solution** I can think of.
✅ This is **the best solution** I can think of.

🧠 中文："这是最好的方案"——中文最高级用"最 + 形容词"，不需要额外的冠词。学习者认为"best"本身就表达了"最"，忘记 the 是必须的。同样：序数词（the first, the second）和 only 之前也需要 the（the only way）。

🔧 记住"唯一/极端/排序 = the"：只要表达"唯一的、最好的、最坏的、第一个、最后一个"，就加 the。

✏️ 填空：
1. This is ___ most challenging project I've worked on.
2. She was ___ first person to raise the concern.
3. There's only one viable option, and ___ only option is to rollback.

---

**Error 5 — 复数泛指加 the (The + Plural for General Reference)**

❌ **The microservices** are popular in modern architecture.
✅ **Microservices** are popular in modern architecture.

🧠 中文：同 Error 2——中文没有冠词，学习者怕"裸奔"习惯加 the。规则：复数名词用于泛指整个类别时，用零冠词 ∅。"Microservices"泛指所有微服务。但如果是特指：**The microservices** in our system are written in Go —— 这里的 the 指"我们系统中的那些微服务"，是特指，所以加 the。

🔧 测试法：把句子翻译成中文。如果"微服务在当代架构中流行"听起来自然（不特指某一批微服务），就用零冠词。如果听起来像是特指某几个—— "那些微服务"——就用 the。

✏️ 判断是否需要 the：
1. (∅ / The) Engineers should write clean code.
2. (∅ / The) engineers on our team write clean code.
3. (∅ / The) AI models require large amounts of data.

---

**Error 6 — 独一无二的事物遗漏 the (Missing the for Unique Entities)**

❌ **Sun rises in east.**
✅ **The sun rises in the east.**

🧠 中文："太阳从东边升起"——学习者直接把词语拼接，因为中文不需要冠词来表示"唯一性"。英语中，世界上独一无二的事物必须加 the：the sun, the moon, the earth, the internet, the government, the environment。

🔧 清单记忆：凡是"全世界只有一个"的——天体、环境、政府、互联网——都加 the。

✏️ 改错：
1. Moon was bright last night.
2. Internet has changed the way we work.
3. We need to protect environment.

---

**Error 7 — 学科/语言/运动前加 the (The Before Academic Subjects/Languages/Sports)**

❌ She is good at **the mathematics**. / I want to learn **the English**.
✅ She is good at **mathematics**. / I want to learn **English**.

🧠 中文："她擅长数学"——问题同 Error 2，过度补偿。规则：学科（mathematics, physics, computer science）、语言（English, Chinese, Python）、运动（basketball, swimming）不加 the。

🔧 记住例外：学科、语言、运动都不需要冠词。唯一的混淆点：the English = 英格兰人/英国人（复数集合）；English = 英语。

✏️ 改错：
1. He is studying the computer science at university.
2. I practice the basketball every Sunday.
3. The Python is a popular programming language.

---

**Error 8 — 有 of 限定但没有 the (Missing The Before Noun + of Phrase)**

❌ **Quality of code** is important.
✅ **The quality of code** is important. (如果特指某段代码的质量)
✅ **Code quality** is important. (泛指编码质量)

🧠 中文："代码质量很重要"——中文用"的"连接，不区分泛指和特指。英语中：当一个名词后有 of 短语限定时，往往是特指，需要 the：the quality of the code, the beginning of the project, the root of the problem。但如果你用复合名词形式（code quality），就不需要冠词。

🔧 规律：X of Y → the X of Y（绝大多数情况）。如果你不喜欢用 the，可以把 of 短语改为复合名词：code quality, project start, problem root（但不总是自然）。

✏️ 选择正确的形式：
1. (Quality of the code / The quality of the code / Code quality) in this repo varies.
2. (Beginning of the project / The beginning of the project) was smooth.
3. We need to identify (root cause of the issue / the root cause of the issue).

### 1.2 介词错误 (Preposition Errors) — 8 mistakes

英文介词是中文学习者犯错率第二高的领域。中文的介词系统比英语简单得多——同一个汉字"在"可以覆盖英语的 at/in/on；许多英语介词在中文中根本没有对应的词。

---

**Error 9 — discuss about / emphasize on / reach to (及物动词后多加介词)**

❌ We need to **discuss about** the architecture.
✅ We need to **discuss** the architecture.

🧠 中文："讨论关于架构"——"关于"在中文中常常出现，学习者映射为 about。但英语中 discuss 是及物动词，直接加宾语。同样的错误：emphasize on（correct: emphasize），reach to（correct: reach，但 reach out to 是对的），mention about（correct: mention），contact with（correct: contact）。

🔧 记一张"伪不及物动词"黑名单——这些词后不需要介词：
- discuss (NOT discuss about)
- emphasize (NOT emphasize on)
- reach (NOT reach to)
- mention (NOT mention about)
- contact (NOT contact with)
- approach (NOT approach to)
- request (NOT request for)
- await (NOT await for)

✏️ 删除多余的介词：
1. We discussed about the timeline during the meeting.
2. The manager emphasized on the importance of testing.
3. You can contact with me via Slack.

---

**Error 10 — depend of / belong of / consist from (介词搭配错误)**

❌ The success of the project depends **of** the team's collaboration.
✅ The success of the project depends **on** the team's collaboration.

🧠 中文："成功取决于团队合作"——"于"在中文中可以对应多个英文介词（on, upon, from），学习者随意选择。但英文中动词/形容词+介词的搭配是固定的，必须逐个记忆：

| 表达 | ✅ 正确搭配 | ❌ 常见错误 |
|------|----------|-----------|
| 取决于 | depend on / rely on | depend of |
| 属于 | belong to | belong of |
| 由…组成 | consist of | consist from/with |
| 对…感兴趣 | interested in | interested about |
| 对于…负责 | responsible for | responsible of |
| 擅长 | good at | good in |
| 与…不同 | different from | different with |
| 参与 | participate in | participate to |
| 申请 | apply for (工作/许可) / apply to (学校/机构) | apply to (工作) |
| 到达 | arrive at (小地方) / arrive in (大地方) | arrive to |

🔧 没有捷径——必须像背单词一样背动词+介词搭配。建议用 Anki 句子卡：正面写中文，反面写完整英语句子（动词+介词加粗）。

✏️ 改错：
1. The result depends of many factors.
2. I'm not agree with your opinion. (hint: agree 不是名词)
3. This PR consists from three independent changes.

---

**Error 11 — 时间介词混淆 (Time Preposition Confusion: at/in/on)**

❌ I'll finish it **in** Monday. / The meeting starts **at** July.
✅ I'll finish it **on** Monday. / The meeting starts **in** July.

🧠 中文："在星期一"、"在七月"——中文统一用一个"在"字，不问后面是具体日子还是月份。英语严格区分 at（点）/ on（日/面）/ in（段/内）：

| 介词 | 规则 | 示例 |
|------|------|------|
| **at** | 精确时间点、节日（无 day 的） | at 3pm, at noon, at midnight, at Christmas |
| **on** | 具体某一天、星期、日期 | on Monday, on May 12, on Christmas Day, on the weekend (US) |
| **in** | 月份、年份、季节、较长时段 | in May, in 2026, in summer, in the morning, in two weeks |

🔧 记住梯级：at < on < in（时间精度递减）。最精确用 at（几点钟），日级用 on（哪一天），月级以上用 in（哪个月/年/季）。

✏️ 填空（at / on / in）：
1. The deployment is scheduled ___ Friday evening ___ 10pm.
2. We launched the product ___ March ___ 2025.
3. See you ___ noon ___ Monday.

---

**Error 12 — 遗漏介词 (Missing Preposition)**

❌ We discussed **the issue** the meeting.
✅ We discussed the issue **at** / **during** the meeting.

❌ I'll reply back **you** tomorrow.
✅ I'll reply back **to** you tomorrow. / I'll get back **to** you tomorrow.

🧠 中文："我们会议上讨论了"——中文用"上"但往往省略，因为语序本身就表达了"在哪里"。"我回复你"——"你"直接跟在动词后，无需介词。英语必须在名词前加介词表示地点/时间/方向关系；许多英语动词（reply, explain, listen, wait, apply）后需要介词才能接宾语。

🔧 每次你写 [动词 + 名词] 组合时，问自己："这两个名词之间是什么关系？"如果是地点/时间/方向/接受者关系——大概率需要介词。

常见遗漏介词的动词：
- reply to someone / reply to an email
- explain to someone (NOT explain someone)
- listen to someone
- wait for someone
- apply for a job
- look at something
- laugh at something

✏️ 在以下句子中插入必要的介词：
1. Please reply ___ my email by Friday.
2. Can you explain ___ the team how the algorithm works?
3. I've been waiting ___ this update for a week.

---

**Error 13 — 空间介词混淆 (Spatial Preposition Confusion)**

❌ I was **in** the meeting room. (when meaning "at a specific location")
✅ I was **in** the meeting room. (inside the room, correct!)
❌ I'll meet you **in** the entrance. (at the entrance is correct)
✅ I'll meet you **at** the entrance.

🧠 中文：统一用"在"表达所有空间位置。"我在会议室"和"我在入口等你"都用的同一个"在"字。英语中的空间介词区分远比中文细致：

| 介词 | 空间含义 | 示例 |
|------|---------|------|
| **at** | 定位为一个"点"——不强调在内部 | at the entrance, at the desk, at the bus stop |
| **in** | 在三维空间内部 | in the room, in the building, in the box |
| **on** | 在表面上；在楼层/道路上 | on the table, on the 3rd floor, on Main Street |

最容易混淆的三组：
- **at the corner** (路口/街头 — 点状位置) vs **in the corner** (房间的角落 — 内部空间)
- **in the way** (挡住道路) vs **on the way** (在途中) vs **by the way** (顺便说一下)
- **on time** (准时) vs **in time** (及时赶到)

🔧 视觉化：把 at 想象成地图上的一个图钉（点）；in 想象成一个立体的箱子（内部）；on 想象成一张平的桌面（表面）。

✏️ 填空（at / in / on）：
1. Let's meet ___ the lobby ___ the 5th floor.
2. She's waiting ___ the bus stop.
3. There's a whiteboard ___ the wall.

---

**Error 14 — "跟…有关" / "关于…"之间的 confusing prepositions (about/on/regarding/regard to)**

❌ I have a question **regarding to** the API design.
✅ I have a question **regarding** the API design / **about** the API design / **concerning** the API design.

🧠 中文："关于"是一个万能的词，覆盖 about, on, regarding, concerning, with regard to 等所有英文表达。但在英文中：
- regarding = about（不加 to）
- concerning = about（不加 to）
- **with regard to** / **in regard to**（有 to，有 regard — 注意是 regard 不是 regards）
- **as regards**（有 s，没有 to）
- discuss on the API（错误 — discuss 是及物动词）

🔧 记住三条规则：
1. regarding = about → 不加 to
2. with regard to / in regard to → 有 to，有 regard（单数）
3. discuss + 直接宾语 → 不加 on/about

✏️ 改错：
1. I'd like to ask a question regarding to the deployment process.
2. We need to discuss about the incident during standup.
3. With regards to your proposal, I have some concerns.

---

**Error 15 — 介词后动词用不定式 (Infinitive Instead of Gerund After Preposition)**

❌ I'm looking forward **to hear** from you.
✅ I'm looking forward **to hearing** from you.

🧠 中文：动词不分形式（"听到"永远都是"听到"，不会变成"听到-ing"）。学习者习惯性地在介词后使用动词原形。但英语规则非常明确：**介词后的动词永远是 -ing 形式**。唯一看起来例外的是 "look forward to" 中的 to 是介词（不是不定式标志），所以 "to doing" 是对的。

🔧 关键判断：to 是不定式标志还是介词？
- 不定式 to → 动词原形：I want to go, I need to finish
- 介词 to → 动词 -ing：look forward to doing, be used to doing, be committed to doing, object to doing

常见的"介词 to + doing"：
- look forward to doing
- be used to / accustomed to doing (习惯于)
- be committed to / dedicated to doing
- object to doing (反对)
- in addition to doing

✏️ 改错（注意：有些句子是正确的）：
1. I'm looking forward to work with the new team.
2. She is used to write code in a functional style.
3. We are committed to deliver the feature by Friday.
4. I want to discuss the issue with you. (这个是对的吗？)

---

**Error 16 — "跟…相关"的各种介词 (related to / relevant to / associated with)**

❌ This is not related **with** the bug we found.
✅ This is not related **to** the bug we found.

🧠 中文："跟.bug 有关"——中文"跟"可以映射为 with/to/about，学习者不确定该用哪一个。这三个表达各有固定搭配：

| 表达 | 正确搭配 | 
|------|---------|
| related | related **to** |
| relevant | relevant **to** |
| associated | associated **with** |
| connected | connected **to** / **with** |
| linked | linked **to** |
| correlated | correlated **with** |

🔧 分组记：
- -ed to 组：related to, linked to, tied to, bound to
- -ed with 组：associated with, correlated with, coupled with, paired with

✏️ 选择正确的介词：
1. The latency spike appears to be related ___ the deployment.
2. Each ticket should be associated ___ a sprint.
3. Is this configuration change relevant ___ the performance issue?

### 1.3 时态与体态错误 (Tense & Aspect Errors) — 8 mistakes

中文不通过动词变形来表达时间关系，所以时态是中国学习者最根本的挑战之一。以下八条都是高频错误。

---

**Error 17 — 第三人称单数遗漏 -s (Missing 3rd Person Singular -s)**

❌ The system **send** a request to the server every 30 seconds.
✅ The system **sends** a request to the server every 30 seconds.

🧠 中文："系统每 30 秒发送一次请求"——动词"发送"不受主语人称和单复数影响。中文动词没有任何变形，所以学习者在写英语时很容易忘记 -s。这是写到一半就忘掉的错误。

🔧 在你的写作检查清单中加入一行："每句话找主语，主语是 he/she/it/单数名词吗？是 → 动词加 -s。"反复练写 he/she/it 开头的句子，直到加上 -s 变成肌肉记忆。

✏️ 改错（找所有遗漏的 -s）：
1. The monitoring dashboard display real-time metrics.
2. My manager expect a weekly report from each engineer.
3. The CI pipeline run automatically on every push.

---

**Error 18 — 一般现在时与现在进行时混淆 (Simple Present vs. Present Continuous)**

❌ I **work** on a new feature right now.
✅ I **am working** on a new feature right now.

🧠 中文："我现在在做新功能"——中文通过加"在"或"正在"表达进行，但动词不变形。学习者常常用最熟悉的简单形式。

核心区别：
- **Simple Present** — 常态/习惯/事实：I work remotely. (always, as a general arrangement)
- **Present Continuous** — 此刻正在进行/现阶段在进行：I am working on a new feature. (right now / these days)

在技术沟通中容易混淆的场景：
- "The service is running" (it's running right now) vs "The service runs every hour" (scheduled)
- "I write documentation" (I do it habitually) vs "I am writing documentation" (I'm doing it these days)

🔧 问自己：这个动作是"常态/规律"还是"此刻/当下在发生"？规律用 simple，当下用 continuous。

✏️ 选择正确的形式：
1. I (write / am writing) an incident report right now.
2. The cron job (runs / is running) every morning at 3am.
3. She (works / is working) on the authentication module this sprint.

---

**Error 19 — 现在完成时使用不足 (Underuse of Present Perfect)**

❌ I **lived** in Shanghai **for** 5 years. (If you still live there now, or if it's relevant to now)
⚠️ 这个句子本身语法对——但含义变了：一般过去时 "I lived in Shanghai for 5 years" 暗示你已经不再住在上海了，那个时期已经结束。

如果你现在仍然住在上海：✅ I **have lived** in Shanghai **for** 5 years. (现在完成时 — 从过去开始持续到现在)

🧠 中文："我在上海住了 5 年了"——中文用"了"表达完成，但这个词也可以用于过去，所以中文不强制区分"持续到现在"和"已经结束"。英文严格区分：

| 时态 | 含义 | 示例 |
|------|------|------|
| Past Simple | 动作在过去结束，与现在无关 | I lived in Shanghai. (now I don't) |
| Present Perfect | 动作在过去发生，结果/影响持续到现在 | I have lived in Shanghai for 5 years. (still here) |
| Present Perfect | 经历（不强调时间） | I have deployed to production many times. |
| Present Perfect | 刚完成（just/already/yet） | The build has just finished. |

🔧 判断标准：如果"过去的事对现在有影响/关联/持续"，就用现在完成时。这在技术沟通中尤其重要：
- "We have identified the root cause" (implies: we know it NOW) vs "We identified the root cause" (at some point in the past, maybe no longer relevant)
- "Have you pushed the fix?" (is it done NOW, I want the result) vs "Did you push the fix?" (at that specific time)

✏️ 选择正确时态：
1. I (worked / have worked) here since 2022. (I still work here.)
2. We (deployed / have deployed) the hotfix an hour ago, and the system (recovered / has recovered).
3. She (never used / has never used) Kubernetes before.

---

**Error 20 — 过去完成时使用不足 (Underuse of Past Perfect)**

❌ When I checked the logs, the server already **crashed**.
✅ When I checked the logs, the server **had already crashed**.

🧠 中文："我查看日志的时候，服务器已经崩溃了"——中文通过"已经"+"了"来表达时间先后，不改变动词形式。但在英文中，如果要把两个发生在过去的动作按时间先后排序——先发生的用过去完成时，后发生的用一般过去时。

🔧 过去完成是"过去的过去"——想象时间轴：

```
现在 ——— 我查日志 (过去: I checked) ——— 服务器崩溃 (更早: had crashed) ———> 时间
```

判断标准：当你讲一个过去的故事，里面有两个动作一先一后——先的用 past perfect。

技术场景中特别有用的过去完成时：
- "The team had already rolled back before we noticed the issue."
- "By the time the alert fired, the database had been down for 10 minutes."
- "I hadn't realized the impact until I saw the metrics."

✏️ 改写句子，在必要的地方使用过去完成时：
1. When I joined the call, the on-call engineer (already identified) the root cause.
2. The deploy (finish) before the incident was reported.
3. I (never see) a memory leak of that magnitude before that day.

---

**Error 21 — 将来时间从句中的现在时 (Future Time Clauses — Present not Will)**

❌ When the deploy **will finish**, I'll notify the team.
✅ When the deploy **finishes**, I'll notify the team.

🧠 中文："当部署完成的时候，我会通知团队"——中文从句不区分时态，"完成"就是"完成"，学习者自然地在所有动词前都加上 will。但英语规则：**表示将来时间的状语从句中（when, after, before, as soon as, until, if, unless），动词用一般现在时，主句用将来时**。

🔧 公式：When/After/As soon as + 从句 [一般现在时] + 主句 [will/can/may + 动词原形]

但是注意区分：
- 时间状语从句：When the CI pipeline **passes**, we'll merge. (passes, not will pass)
- 宾语从句（名词从句）：I don't know when the CI pipeline **will pass**. (will pass, 这里 when 引导的是 know 的宾语，不是状语)

✏️ 改错：
1. After the tests will pass, we will merge the PR.
2. As soon as the monitoring will detect the anomaly, it will trigger an alert.
3. I'll let you know when the meeting will start. (提示：这里的 when 从句是宾语从句还是状语从句？)

---

**Error 22 — 虚拟条件句中时态后移 (Conditional Tense Confusion)**

❌ If I **will have** time, I will review your PR.
✅ If I **have** time, I will review your PR. (Type 1: real possibility)

❌ If I **am** you, I **will** not merge it now.
✅ If I **were** you, I **would** not merge it now. (Type 2: unreal/imaginary)

🧠 中文："如果我有时间，我会 review"——中文通过"如果/就"表达条件关系，动词不变。英语 Type 1 条件句（真实可能）：If + 一般现在时, 主句 will。Type 2 条件句（与现在事实相反/不太可能）：If + 一般过去时, 主句 would + 动词原形。

**特别容易出错：** 当条件句的主语是你自己时，中文"如果我是你"中文常直接映射为 "If I am you"——但这个句子的本质是"我不可能是你"，所以是 Type 2，要用 If I were you, I would...

🔧 判断：
- 这个条件可能实现吗？→ 可能 → Type 1: If + present, will
- 这个条件不太可能/与事实相反？→ Type 2: If + past, would

✏️ 选择正确时态：
1. If the server (crashes / will crash / crashed), the on-call engineer (gets / will get / would get) paged.
2. If I (am / were / will be) the tech lead, I (will / would) invest in observability first.
3. If we (don't add / didn't add / wouldn't add) rate limiting, the API will be vulnerable to abuse.

---

**Error 23 — 静态动词使用进行时 (Stative Verbs in Continuous Form)**

❌ I **am understanding** your point.
✅ I **understand** your point.

❌ The system **is consisting** of three modules.
✅ The system **consists** of three modules.

🧠 中文："我理解了你的意思"和"我正在理解你的意思"在中文中都可以说。但英语中有一类"静态动词"（stative verbs）——表示状态、感觉、认知、拥有、存在的词——通常不用进行时。

常见静态动词：
- **认知**：know, understand, believe, think (=believe), remember, forget
- **感觉**：like, love, hate, prefer, want, need
- **感官**：see, hear, smell, taste, feel
- **拥有/关系**：have (=own), belong, consist, contain, include, depend
- **存在**：seem, appear, be, exist, matter, mean

⚠️ 例外：many stative verbs have a dynamic meaning too:
- I **think** it's a good idea (stative — believe) vs I **am thinking** about the problem (dynamic — considering)
- I **have** a MacBook (stative — own) vs I **am having** lunch (dynamic — eating) vs I **am having** trouble with the API (dynamic — experiencing)

🔧 当你不确定一个动词是静态还是动态的，问：这个动词描述的是一个"状态"还是一个"动作"？状态 → 不用进行时。

✏️ 改错：
1. I'm not understanding the error message.
2. The team is wanting to adopt Rust for the new service.
3. This container is containing all the dependencies.
4. I'm thinking we should rollback. (这个是错的吗？)

---

**Error 24 — 时态一致性 (Tense Consistency in Narrative)**

❌ She said the feature **is** ready, but when we tested it, we **find** several bugs.
✅ She said the feature **was** ready, but when we tested it, we **found** several bugs.

🧠 中文："她说功能准备好了，但我们测试的时候，发现了几个 bug"——中文动词不表达时间先后，读者通过时间词（"的时候"）自行理解顺序。所以中文母语者常在一个英语段落中时态乱跳：前面过去时，中间跳到现在时，再回到过去时。英文要求在一个叙事段落中保持时态一致性。

🔧 写完后做"时态一致性检查"：在你整段话中，动词之间相互一致吗？

| 场景 | 建议的时态 |
|------|----------|
| 讲过去的事（postmortem, incident report） | 全用过去时；先发生的用过去完成时 |
| 描述系统当前状态 | 全用现在时（一般现在时/现在完成时） |
| 讲一段经历 | 用过去时，直到切换到"现在看法" |

✏️ 改错（统一时态）：
1. We noticed the latency spike at 2pm. We checked the dashboards and the CPU usage is at 90%. We immediately scaled up.
2. The retrospective was productive. Everyone shares their thoughts and we agree on the action items.

### 1.4 语序错误 (Word Order Errors) — 7 mistakes

中文和英文的基本语序都是 SVO，但在很多细节上——状位置、疑问词位置、形容词顺序——有显著差异。

---

**Error 25 — 副词/状语位置不当 (Adverb/Adverbial Placement)**

❌ I **very much like** the proposal.
✅ I like the proposal **very much**.

❌ We **often are** late to the standup.
✅ We **are often** late to the standup.

🧠 中文："我非常喜欢这个提案"——状语通常在动词前面。"我经常迟到"——"经常"在"迟到"前面。中文学习者习惯把所有副词都放在动词前面，但这在英语中经常不对：

英语状语位置规则：
- **频率副词**（often, always, never, sometimes, rarely）→ be 动词后、实义动词前
- **程度副词**（very much, a lot）→ 句末
- **方式副词**（carefully, quickly, well）→ 动词后或句末
- **时间/地点状语**（yesterday, at the office）→ 句末（或句首强调）

🔧 英文状语偏好 **句尾** 位置——这和中文"动词前"形成强烈对比。不确定的时候，把状语放句尾通常不会错。频率副词记特殊规则：be 后实前。

✏️ 改正以下句子的状语位置：
1. I very carefully reviewed the code.
2. She always is on top of her tasks.
3. He explained the architecture to the team in detail yesterday during the meeting.

---

**Error 26 — 嵌入式问句的语序 (Embedded Question Word Order)**

❌ I don't know **what is the root cause**.
✅ I don't know **what the root cause is**.

❌ Can you explain **how does this algorithm work**?
✅ Can you explain **how this algorithm works**?

🧠 中文："我不知道根本原因是什么"——中文问句和陈述句语序一致，没有倒装。所以中国学习者直接按中文语序映射为 "what is the root cause"，但这个语序只在直接问句中是对的。

英语规则：
- **直接问句**：Question word + 助动词 + 主语 + 动词？(What **is** the root cause?)
- **嵌入式问句**（在 "I don't know" / "Can you explain" 之后）：Question word + **主语 + 动词** (what the root cause **is**)

🔧 一个简单的心理模型：嵌入式问句就是"把直接问句变回陈述句的语序"：
- 直接问句：What is the root cause? (be 在主语前)
- 陈述句：... what the root cause is. (be 回到主语后)

✏️ 改写为正确语序：
1. I'm not sure what is the best approach.
2. Could you clarify what does this error message mean?
3. The team was confused about why was the deployment failing.

---

**Error 27 — 形容词顺序 (Adjective Order)**

❌ a **wooden old beautiful desk**
✅ a **beautiful old wooden desk**

🧠 中文："一张漂亮的旧木桌"——中文形容词顺序是"主观评价 + 状态 + 材质"，而英语遵循一个严格但母语者习以为常的规则：**Opinion → Size → Age → Shape → Color → Origin → Material → Purpose → NOUN**（记住首字母：OSASCOMP）。

🔧 虽然你不会经常需要排列一堆形容词（技术文档中用得不多），但当你用了两个以上形容词时——记住：主观在前，客观在后；越本质的越靠近名词。

✏️ 排序以下形容词：
1. a (metal / shiny / new) laptop
2. a (debugging / Python / powerful) tool
3. a (Japanese / young / talented) engineer

---

**Error 28 — "虽然...但是..."与"因为...所以..."的双重连接 (Double Conjunction)**

❌ **Although** the test passed, **but** the coverage was low.
✅ **Although** the test passed, the coverage was low.
✅ The test passed, **but** the coverage was low.

❌ **Because** the server was overloaded, **so** the response was slow.
✅ **Because** the server was overloaded, the response was slow.
✅ The server was overloaded, **so** the response was slow.

🧠 中文："虽然测试通过了，但是覆盖率低。"——中文标准结构就是"虽然...但是..."双连词成对出现。"因为...所以..."同理。学习者在写英语时直接逐词翻译，保留了两个连词。但英语中：一个句子只能有一个连词；从属连词（although, because, if, when...）已经标记了从句关系，不需要再加并列连词（but, so, and）。

🔧 公式：从属连词 + 并列连词 = ❌。用一个，不要两个都用。

✏️ 删除多余的连词：
1. Although the incident was resolved quickly, but we need a postmortem.
2. Because the database migration took longer than expected, so the release was delayed.
3. Even though I disagree with the decision, yet I'll support the team. (y 是什么？和 but 一样的逻辑)

---

**Error 29 — Only/Not only 的位置 (Placement of Only / Not only)**

❌ I **only** fixed one bug yesterday. (修饰的是 fixed？还是 one bug？)
✅ I fixed **only** one bug yesterday. / I fixed one bug **only** yesterday.

🧠 中文："我昨天只修了一个 bug"——"只"放在动词前面，不会产生歧义。但英语中 only 的位置决定它修饰哪一个成分，位置不同含义不同：

- **Only I** fixed the bug. (就我一个人修的，别人没修)
- I **only fixed** the bug. (我只修了这个 bug，没做别的。但口语中常用。)
- I fixed **only** the bug. (强调唯一的目标是这个 bug)
- I fixed the bug **only** yesterday. (昨天才修——强调时间很晚了)

在正式书面英语中，only 应该紧贴在它修饰的词前面。虽口语中常把 only 放动词前（"I only fixed one bug"），但正式写作中最好精确放置。

🔧 问自己：我到底想强调"唯一"的是什么？然后把 only 放在那个词前面。

✏️ 这些句子中的 only 位置对吗？如果不对，它实际上修饰的是哪个词？
1. The cache only stores session data. (stores 还是 session data?)
2. We only deploy to production on Tuesdays. (deploy 还是 Tuesdays?)
3. Only the on-call engineer can approve the rollback.

---

**Error 30 — 主语过长导致的"头重脚轻" (Heavy Subject Before Verb)**

❌ **The fact that the team had not been informed about the API change that was made by another department last week** caused the outage.
✅ The outage was caused by **the fact that the team had not been informed about the API change that was made by another department last week**.

🧠 中文："团队没有被告知...的事实导致了事故"——中文可以接受非常长的主语然后谓语出现在末尾。但英语强烈偏好 **end-weight**——把重的、长的成分放在句末。一个 20 个词的主语后面跟一个 3 个词的谓语，读起来极不自然。

🔧 两个解决方案：
1. **用被动语态**：把重的成分移到句末作为 by 短语
2. **用形式主语 it**：It + is/was + [重成分] + that... (但注意，这是强调句型)

✏️ 改写以下句子，避免"头重脚轻"：
1. Whether we should adopt microservices or stick with the monolith is the question.
2. All the engineers who contributed to the project during the last quarter attended the meeting.

---

**Error 31 — 口语化语序写入正式文档 (Informal Word Order in Formal Writing)**

❌ The problem is **hard to find the root cause**.
✅ **It** is hard **to find the root cause** of the problem.

❌ API rate limiting is **simple to implement**.
✅ **It** is simple **to implement** API rate limiting.

🧠 中文："问题很难找到根本原因"——话题-评论结构（topic-comment）是中文的常规表达："问题"是话题，"很难找到根本原因"是评论。但英语标准结构用 **it 作形式主语**（preparatory "it"），把真正的主语（不定式短语）移到后面：It is hard to find the root cause。

另外注意：The problem is hard to find 在英语中是歧义的——是"问题很难被找到"还是"问题的根源很难找到"？

🔧 正式的 tech writing 中，遇到："X is [adjective] to [verb]"，先想想是不是应该用 "It is [adjective] to [verb] X"。

✏️ 改写以下句子为正式表达：
1. The bug was difficult to reproduce.
2. The architecture is complex to explain in one diagram.
3. Production incidents are stressful to handle without good monitoring.

---

### 1.5 主谓一致错误 (Subject-Verb Agreement) — 4 mistakes

---

**Error 32 — 主语有后置修饰语时动词与修饰语中的名词一致 (Attraction Error — Verb Agrees with Nearest Noun)**

❌ The configuration of the microservices **are** complex.
✅ The configuration (of the microservices) **is** complex.

❌ The list of requirements **have** been updated.
✅ The list (of requirements) **has** been updated.

🧠 中文：不区分单复数动词形式，所以学习者倾向于让动词和离它最近的名词一致——"microservices are"听起来很顺。但真正的主语是 configuration（单数），不是 microservices。

🔧 当你写 [主语 + of/in/with + 名词] 结构时，暂时划掉介词短语再找主语：
- The configuration ~~of the microservices~~ **is** complex.
- The list ~~of requirements~~ **has** been updated.

**特别陷阱：** 主语是抽象名词时尤其容易错——因为抽象名词的单数形式看不出来：
- The **performance** of our APIs (is/are) critical.
- The **reliability** of distributed systems (depends/depend) on proper monitoring.

✏️ 选择正确的动词形式：
1. The quality of our code reviews (has / have) improved.
2. The number of incidents this month (is / are) lower.
3. A set of best practices (was / were) established. (注意："a set" 是单数)

---

**Error 33 — 以 Every/Each/Neither/Either 开头的主语用复数动词**

❌ Every engineer on the team **have** access to the dashboard.
✅ Every engineer on the team **has** access to the dashboard.

🧠 中文："每个工程师"虽然由多个工程师组成，但"每个"强调的是个体——中文不通过动词表达这个区别，学习者看到"工程师"就想用复数动词。但英语中：every, each, either, neither, anyone, someone, nobody 后面的动词永远是单数。

🔧 记：Every/Each/Neither/Either + 名词 (单数或看起来像"组合") + **单数动词**

扩展：
- Every PR and every commit **is** reviewed. (Every X and every Y — 仍是单数！)
- Each of the engineers **has** a different opinion. (of 后面的复数不影响动词)
- Neither the PM nor the engineer **was** at the meeting. (neither...nor 就近一致——但日常中单数最安全)

✏️ 改正错误：
1. Every developer have the right to question the architecture.
2. Each of the servers are running the latest version.
3. Neither the frontend nor the backend team were informed.

---

**Error 34 — 主语从句/不定式主语用复数动词 (Clause/Infinitive Subject with Plural Verb)**

❌ Writing tests **are** important.
✅ Writing tests **is** important.

❌ That the system failed **are** concerning.
✅ That the system failed **is** concerning.

🧠 中文："写测试很重要"——"写测试"三个字没有单复数标记。学习者把 tests 当成主语用复数动词。但真正的主语是整个动名词短语 "Writing tests"（写测试这个行为），不是 tests 这个词。类似地，that 从句作主语时也是单数。

🔧 动词-ing 短语作主语 → 单数；that 从句作主语 → 单数；to 不定式作主语 → 单数：
- Writing tests is important.
- That we didn't catch the bug is worrying.
- To deploy safely is our top priority.

✏️ 选择正确的动词：
1. Reviewing PRs thoroughly (takes / take) time.
2. That the database was not backed up (was / were) a major oversight.
3. To improve latency and to reduce cost (is / are) our goals for Q3.

---

**Error 35 — "One of the + 复数名词"与"the only one of the + 复数名词"的区别**

❌ She is one of the engineers who **works** on the auth system. (语义不对)
✅ She is one of the engineers who **work** on the auth system.

但：
✅ She is the only one of the engineers who **works** on the auth system.

🧠 中文："她是做认证系统的工程师之一"——中文动词无单复数，不体现这个区别。英语中的规则：
- "one of the + 复数名词 + who" → 先行词是复数名词(who = engineers)，所以 who 从句用复数动词
- "the only one of the + 复数名词 + who" → 先行词是 one(who = the only one)，所以 who 从句用单数动词

🔧 判断 who 指谁：问自己——"做这个动作的人是一群人还是唯一的那个人？"
- one of the engineers who **work** → 有多个工程师都做这个工作，她是其中之一
- the only one who **works** → 就她一个人做这个工作

✏️ 选择正确的动词：
1. He is one of the developers who (prefers / prefer) functional programming.
2. She is the only one of the engineers who (has / have) on-call experience this quarter.

---

### 1.6 代词与指称错误 (Pronoun & Reference Errors) — 4 mistakes

---

**Error 36 — it / they 指代不明确 (Ambiguous Antecedent)**

❌ The service calls the database, and when **it** times out, **it** retries **it**.
✅ When the database times out, the service retries the query.

🧠 中文："服务调用数据库，当它超时时，它会重试它"——中文中三个"它"可以通过上下文和常识（数据库会超时，服务会重试）来解歧。而且中文常省略代词：不说"数据库超时"而说"超时"。但英语读者完全不知道这三个 it 分别指什么。这是中文学习者最常见的写作问题之一——代词的过度使用和模糊指代。

🔧 最简单的修复方法：**直接重复名词**。不要怕重复——在技术写作中，清晰 > 简洁。
- "The service calls the database. When **the database** times out, **the service** retries **the query**."

✏️ 改写以下句子，消除歧义的代词：
1. The frontend sends the token to the API, and it validates it before it processes the request.
2. When the manager spoke to the engineer about the deadline, he seemed frustrated. (谁 frustrated？)
3. We need to fix the bug in the authentication module because it is critical.

---

**Error 37 — 单数 they 与性别假设 (Gender Assumption in Technical Writing)**

❌ When a user logs in, **he** should see the dashboard.
✅ When a user logs in, **they** should see the dashboard.
✅ When users log in, **they** should see the dashboard.

🧠 中文：口语中"他"可泛指男女性（书面倾向于"他/她"）。但英语中 he 代表所有人已经过时了。现代技术写作中，用 they 作单数泛指是标准做法。或者改用复数主语。

🔧 三种可接受的写法（按推荐程度排序）：
1. 改用复数：When users log in, they should see...
2. 用单数 they：When a user logs in, they should see...
3. 用 he or she / s/he：不推荐——太啰嗦

多数现代风格指南（Google, Microsoft, Apple）都支持单数 they。

✏️ 改写为性别中立的表达：
1. Each developer should commit his code daily.
2. When a project manager sees a blocker, he should escalate immediately.
3. The engineer is responsible for the quality of his output.

---

**Error 38 — 物主代词与冠词的混淆 (Possessive vs. Article Confusion)**

❌ I hurt **the** leg. / She raised **the** hand.
✅ I hurt **my** leg. / She raised **her** hand.

🧠 中文："我伤了腿"、"她举起了手"——中文中身体部位通常用"的"或零标记，"我的腿"、"她的手"的"我的"和"她的"在口语中常省略。学习者将这种省略习惯带到英语中，用 the 代替物主代词。但英语中身体部位和所属物品必须加物主代词（my, your, his, her, our）。

同样的问题也出现在物品上：
- ❌ I left the laptop at office. → ✅ I left **my** laptop at **the** office.

🔧 如果某物属于某人——用物主代词，不要用 the。

✏️ 改正以下句子：
1. After the accident, she went to hospital to check the back. (back 是谁的？)
2. I need to charge the phone before the meeting.
3. He spilled coffee on the keyboard.

---

**Error 39 — 遗漏关系代词 (Missing Relative Pronoun)**

❌ The engineer **reviewed my PR** gave very constructive feedback.
✅ The engineer **who reviewed** my PR gave very constructive feedback.

🧠 中文："review 我 PR 的那个工程师"——中文可以用动词短语直接修饰名词（"审核我 PR 的"），不需要关系词。所以学习者写英语时，容易直接把动词短语堆在名词后面，忘记英语必须有关系代词（who/that/which）来引导定语从句。

这在口语中可能被听懂，但在书面英语中是不规范的。

🔧 寻找 [名词 + 动词短语] 的搭配——如果动词短语在"解释/修饰"那个名词，中间必须加关系代词。

特别容易出错的场景：
- ❌ The issue we found **is caused by** a misconfiguration. → ✅ The issue **that** we found is caused by a misconfiguration. (这里 that 在从句中作宾语，口语中可省略，但正式写作建议保留)
- ❌ We need someone **knows** Kubernetes inside out. → ✅ We need someone **who knows** Kubernetes inside out.

✏️ 插入缺失的关系代词：
1. The team developed the feature was recognized at the all-hands.
2. I read a blog post explains the tradeoff really well.
3. The incident we discussed in the retrospective has been resolved.

### 1.7 词汇与搭配错误 (Vocabulary & Collocation Errors) — 6 mistakes

---

**Error 40 — learn knowledge / teach knowledge (直接翻译的搭配错误)**

❌ Students come to school to **learn knowledge**.
✅ Students come to school to **acquire / gain knowledge**.

❌ The workshop **taught me many knowledges**.
✅ The workshop **taught me a lot** / **imparted a great deal of knowledge**.

🧠 中文："学习知识"是最自然的搭配。学习者直接映射为 "learn knowledge"。但在英语中，"learn" 和 "knowledge" 不搭配——你 learn 的是学科、技能或具体内容，不 learn "knowledge"。这种搭配属于中式英语（Chinglish）。

以下全是中式英语搭配：

| ❌ 中式搭配 | ✅ 自然英语 |
|------------|----------|
| learn knowledge | acquire/gain knowledge |
| teach knowledge | impart knowledge / teach skills |
| increase my knowledge | broaden/deepen my knowledge |
| master knowledge | master a subject / gain expertise |
| do exercise | exercise / work out |
| make a decision (正确！) | make a decision ✅ (这个是对的，但要区别于 "do a decision") |

🔧 对于任何中文常用搭配，不要假设直接翻译就行。查一下搭配词典（如 Ozdic.com）或搜索 "[word] collocations"。

✏️ 选择自然的英语表达：
1. Through this project, I (learned / gained) a lot of practical knowledge about distributed systems.
2. Reading technical blogs (increases / broadens) my understanding of the industry.
3. The mentor (taught / shared) her experience with the team.

---

**Error 41 — open/close 的滥用 (Overuse of open/close for everything)**

❌ Please **open** the light. / Please **close** the light.
✅ Please **turn on** the light. / Please **turn off** the light.

❌ **Open** the computer. / **Close** the file.
✅ **Turn on** the computer. / **Close** the file (正确！).

🧠 中文："开灯/关灯"、"开机/关机"、"开电脑/关电脑"、"打开文件/关闭文件"——几乎所有的"开启/关闭"动作都用"开/关"表达。学习者直接映射到 open/close，但英语根据不同对象使用不同动词：

| 对象 | 开启 | 关闭 |
|------|------|------|
| 灯/电器 | turn on / switch on | turn off / switch off |
| 电脑/设备 | turn on / start up / power on | turn off / shut down / power off |
| 应用程序 | open / launch / start | close / quit / exit |
| 文件 | open | close |
| 开关/水龙头 | turn on | turn off |
| 会议/活动 | open / kick off / start | close / wrap up / end |

🔧 两类区别：
- 有物理开关/电流的（灯、电视、空调、电脑）→ turn on / turn off
- 软件层面的（文件、窗口、应用）→ open / close

✏️ 选择正确的动词：
1. Could you (open / turn on) the projector for the presentation?
2. I can't (close / turn off) this application—it's frozen.
3. Let me (open / turn on) the terminal.

---

**Error 42 — "方便"的错译 (Convenient — Subject vs Object Confusion)**

❌ **I am convenient** to use this library.
✅ **It is convenient** for me to use this library. / This library is **convenient to use**.

🧠 中文："我很方便用这个库"——中文可以说"我很方便"、"这个库很好用"，不严格区分主语是"我"还是"东西"。但英语中 convenient 的主语必须是"事情"或"it"——不能说 "I am convenient"（除非你的意思是"我很方便被利用"）。

英语中表达方便/不方便的几种方式：
- **It** is convenient **for someone to do** something.
- Something is convenient **to use** / **to work with**.
- Something is **user-friendly** / **ergonomic** (如果是物理便利)
- ❌ I am convenient to... (错误)

同样的逻辑适用于：difficult, easy, hard, possible, necessary 等形容词：
- ❌ I am difficult to finish this. → ✅ It is difficult for me to finish this.

🔧 永远不要把 "I"、"he"、"she" 作为 convenient/easy/difficult 的主语（除非你字面意思是指"这个人很容易被拿到/很方便"）。

✏️ 改正以下句子（找出主语错误）：
1. I am convenient to join the meeting at 3pm.
2. Are you convenient to discuss this now?
3. The developers are difficult to adopt the new framework.

---

**Error 43 — "有机会做某事"的错译 (Opportunity — I have the vs. There is)**

❌ **There is an opportunity to** this position.
✅ **I have the opportunity to** apply for this position. / This position is an opportunity for me.

❌ **There is a possibility that** the server crashes. (想表达"服务器可能崩溃")
✅ **There is a chance that** the server might crash. / The server **could** crash.

🧠 中文："有机会"和"有可能"都带"有"，学习者常用 there is/are 来翻译。但英语中：
- opportunity 和 chance 可以用 there is（There is an opportunity for growth），但更常跟 "have"
- 表达"可能发生的风险"最好用 could/may/might + verb，而不是用 "there is a possibility that"（太啰嗦）

🔧 三个层次的"可能性"表达：
1. 个人有机会 → I have the opportunity/chance **to do**
2. 客观存在机会 → There is an opportunity **for** / **to**
3. 纯技术可能性 → could / may / might + verb (最简洁)

✏️ 改为更自然的英语：
1. There is an opportunity that this bug appears in production.
2. I'm lucky to have an opportunity to work on this challenging project.
3. There is a chance that the deployment may fail. (太啰嗦了)

---

**Error 44 — 中文量词直接翻译 (Direct Translation of Chinese Measure Words)**

❌ This is **a good news**. / I have **a bread** for breakfast.
✅ This is **good news**. / I have **some bread** / **a piece of bread** for breakfast.

🧠 中文："一条好消息"、"一个面包"——中文所有名词前都可以加量词，学习者把所有量词都直接映射为 a/an。但在英语中，news 是不可数名词（应该说 some news, a piece of news，不是 a news）；bread 也是不可数（a piece of bread, some bread，不是 a bread）。

其他常见不可数名词被当作可数使用的错误：
- ❌ an advice → ✅ a piece of advice / some advice
- ❌ an equipment → ✅ a piece of equipment
- ❌ a furniture → ✅ a piece of furniture / some furniture
- ❌ a research → ✅ some research / a piece of research / a study
- ❌ a progress → ✅ progress / some progress
- ❌ a work → ✅ a piece of work / a job / some work
- ❌ a software → ✅ some software / a piece of software
- ❌ a data → ✅ a piece of data / data (collectively)

🔧 不确定一个名词是否可数时，在词典中查它的 [C] / [U] 标记。或者用 some 代替 a/an，some 对可数和不可数都安全。

✏️ 改错：
1. That's a great news! Congratulations!
2. We need to buy a new equipment for the lab.
3. I found a very useful research on this topic.

---

**Error 45 — "安排/组织"的错译 (arrange vs. organize vs. schedule)**

❌ I will **arrange a meeting** for next week. (这个本身不算太糟，但母语者更常说：)
✅ I will **set up a meeting** / **schedule a meeting** for next week.

❌ Let me **organize my thoughts** and get back to you. (这个可以，但 distinguish)
✅ Let me **organize my thoughts** and get back to you. ✅

🧠 中文："安排会议"、"组织活动"——"安排"和"组织"的语义范围比英文对应词更广：

| 中文 | 不可直接映射的词 | 推荐英文 |
|------|---------------|--------|
| 安排会议 | arrange a meeting (可接受) | set up / schedule a meeting |
| 安排时间 | arrange a time (可接受) | find a time / pick a time |
| 安排面试 | arrange an interview (可接受) | schedule / line up an interview |
| 组织活动 | organize an event ✅ | organize / plan / run |
| 组织架构 | organizational structure ✅ | org structure |

🔧 arrange 和 organize 并不总是错的——但母语者更倾向于更具体的动词。在 Slack/邮件中，"set up" 和 "schedule" 比 "arrange" 更常见。

✏️ 选择最自然的英语表达（有时不止一个正确答案）：
1. I'll (arrange / set up / schedule) a calendar invite for the retrospective.
2. Can we (arrange / find / pick) a time to pair on this?

### 1.8 句式与逻辑错误 (Sentence Structure & Logic) — 4 mistakes

---

**Error 46 — 逗号连接两个完整句子 (Comma Splice)**

❌ The build passed, the deployment failed.
✅ The build passed, **but** the deployment failed.
✅ The build passed. The deployment failed.
✅ The build passed; the deployment failed.
✅ The build passed — the deployment failed.

🧠 中文："构建通过了，部署失败了"——中文用逗号连接两个独立分句是完全正常的。许多学习者直接把这种书写习惯带到英语中。但在英语中，逗号不能用来直接连接两个独立的完整句子（这叫 comma splice），必须用连词、分号、句号或破折号。

🔧 四个修复方案：
1. 加并列连词：..., **and/but/or/so** ...
2. 改为句号：**.** (新句子开头大写)
3. 用分号：**;**
4. 用破折号：**—** (更口语化/风格化)

✏️ 修正 comma splice：
1. The incident was resolved within 30 minutes, we still need a postmortem.
2. She prefers Rust, I prefer Go.
3. We rolled back the deploy, the database migration had already run, we had to manually revert it.

---

**Error 47 — 悬垂分词 (Dangling Modifier / Dangling Participle)**

❌ **Deploying to production**, the error rate suddenly spiked.
(谁在部署？不是 error rate。所以 deploying 没有逻辑主语——"悬垂")

✅ **While we were deploying** to production, the error rate suddenly spiked.
✅ **After the deployment to production**, the error rate suddenly spiked.

❌ **Written in Rust**, the team found the new service very performant.
(谁被用 Rust 写的？不是 team，是 service。Written 的隐含主语应该是 service。)

✅ **Written in Rust**, the new service was very performant. (主语匹配了)
✅ The new service, **which was written in Rust**, was very performant.

🧠 中文："部署到生产环境时，错误率突然飙升"——中文中动名词/分词短语的隐含主语可以自由地从上下文推断，允许"悬垂"。但英语要求分词短语（-ing 或 -ed）的隐含主语必须与主句的主语一致。如果不一致，修饰语就是"悬垂的"（dangling），造成逻辑错误。

🔧 写完每个以 -ing 或 -ed 开头的短语后，立刻检查：主句的主语是不是这个短语的逻辑主语？

测试：把分词短语放到主句主语后面，读一下：
- "Deploying to production, the error rate..." → The error rate was deploying. ❌ 不对！
- "Written in Rust, the team..." → The team was written in Rust. ❌ 不对！

✏️ 修正以下悬垂修饰语：
1. Looking at the dashboards, the latency issue was obvious.
2. Based on the data, the conclusion was drawn by the team.
3. When using the API without authentication, an error will be thrown.

---

**Error 48 — 两个动词罗列 (Serial Verb Construction)**

❌ I went to the office **attend** a meeting.
✅ I went to the office **to attend** a meeting.

❌ He stayed up late **fix** the bug.
✅ He stayed up late **to fix** the bug. / He stayed up late **fixing** the bug.

🧠 中文："我去办公室参加了一个会"——中文允许连动结构（serial verb construction），一个句子中多个动词可以并列出现，用上下文表达目的或方式。英语不允许两个谓语动词直接罗列；必须用一个标记来明确关系——通常是不定式（to do，表示目的）或 and。

🔧 英语中连接两个动词的三种方式：
1. **to + 动词原形**（表目的）：I went to the office **to attend** a meeting.
2. **and + 动词**（表并列）：I went to the office **and attended** a meeting.
3. **动词-ing**（表伴随/同时）：I stayed up late **fixing** the bug.

最常见的是缺 to 的不定式——在英语中，to 不是"可选的"，它是目的关系的必要标记。

✏️ 改错：
1. She opened the IDE wrote some code.
2. I'd like to schedule a meeting discuss the proposal.
3. The team gathered in the conference room reviewed the incident timeline.

---

**Error 49 — 话题化的名词短语 (Topic-Comment as Subject-Predicate)**

❌ **This problem**, we have discussed many times in the retrospective.
✅ **We have discussed this problem** many times in the retrospective.

❌ **The production environment**, deployment requires extra caution.
✅ **Deploying to the production environment** requires extra caution.

🧠 中文是"话题突出型语言"（topic-prominent）——常说"这个问题，我们讨论过很多次了"——先把话题放在句首，再对这个话题进行评论。这种结构在中文中非常自然，但在英语中不自然。英语是"主语突出型语言"（subject-prominent），主语-谓语是基本结构。虽然口语中有左移位（left-dislocation），但在正式写作中不常用。

🔧 写英语句子时，确保句子以**语法主语**开头（做动作的人/事物），而不是以"话题"开头。如果发现自己写的是 [名词 + 逗号 + 完整句子]，停下来——这大概率是话题化结构。

例外：有一些合法的"话题化"结构，但它们在英语中有特定标记（as for, regarding, when it comes to...）：
- **As for the production environment**, deployment requires extra caution.
- **Regarding this problem**, we have discussed it many times.

✏️ 改写以下句子，取消话题化：
1. The performance issue, we need to investigate more deeply.
2. This kind of architecture, it's very hard to debug.
3. The meeting schedule, I'll update it by end of day.

### 1.9 语用与风格错误 (Pragmatics & Style) — 3 mistakes

---

**Error 50 — "Please" 的过度使用使请求听起来突兀 (Overuse of Please Making Requests Sound Abrupt)**

❌ **Please** update the document by Friday.
⚠️ 语法没错，但文化上可能听起来像命令。如果一个美国同事这样说，语气可能已经有点"我不高兴了"。

✅ **Could you** update the document by Friday?
✅ **Would you mind** updating the document by Friday?
✅ **When you get a chance**, could you update the document?

🧠 中文："请"是一个礼貌用词，几乎可以加在任何祈使句前面来表达礼貌。但英语中 please + 祈使句 本身不够软化请求——在日常职场沟通中，英语母语者更倾向于用疑问句（Could you...? Would you...?）而不是直接祈使句来提出请求。

职场沟通的礼貌度阶梯：
1. Update the doc. (命令 — 只在紧急或上下级允许时用)
2. Please update the doc. (礼貌命令 — 听起来可能比较直接)
3. Can you update the doc? (一般请求)
4. Could you update the doc? (礼貌请求 — 推荐日常用)
5. Would you mind updating the doc? (非常礼貌)
6. When you get a chance, could you...? (最缓和)

🔧 在日常合作中，默认用 Could you...? 或 Can you...? 取代 please + 祈使句。

✏️ 改写以下命令为更自然的请求：
1. Please send me the report.
2. Please reply to the thread.
3. Please review this PR when you're free.

---

**Error 51 — 中文"废话开头"直接翻译 (Direct Translation of Chinese Discourse Fillers)**

❌ **As we all know**, the deadline is approaching.
(听起来像是在说：大家都知道的事你为什么还没做？)

✅ The deadline is approaching, **so I'd like to check in on progress**.

❌ **We all think that** / **Everyone agrees that** ...
(可能听起来像你在帮别人发言)

✅ **I think** ... / **From my perspective** ... / **My understanding is that** ...

🧠 中文口语/写作中常有"我们都知道"、"大家一致认为"、"毫无疑问"之类的修辞性开头——它们往往是中文话语组织的方式,而不是字面意思。但直接翻译成英语后，这些表达可能听起来主观断言、预设立场，甚至有点居高临下。

常见陷阱：
- "As we all know" = 听起来像 "你连这个都不知道吗？"
- "We all think that" = 你是谁替所有人说话？证据在哪？
- "Obviously" = 如果事情那么明显，为什么你还需要说出来？
- "Without a doubt" = 用在技术讨论中不必要地戏剧化

🔧 去掉这些 filler——直接说你的观点，或者在前面加上 "I think" / "From my perspective"。技术写作的核心是清晰和准确，不是修辞。

✏️ 改写以下句子为更自然的技术英语：
1. As we all know, testing is important for software quality.
2. Obviously, we need to fix the bug before the release.
3. Everyone agrees that code review should be mandatory.

---

**Error 52 — 邮件/文档结尾的中式客套 (Email/Doc Closings with Chinese Formulas)**

❌ **That's all. Thank you.**
⚠️ 语法没问题，但听起来很突然——像你在说"我说完了"然后立即结束对话。

✅ **Let me know if you have any questions.**
✅ **Looking forward to your feedback.**
✅ **Thanks for your time!**
✅ **Happy to discuss further if needed.**

❌ **I'm sorry for bothering you.** / **Sorry for the interruption.**
(每次沟通都道歉会削弱你的自信)
✅ **Thanks for taking a look.** (正面表达)

🧠 中文邮件常以"以上就是我的汇报"或"打扰了"结束——这是中式客套。直接翻译为英语（"That's all. Thank you."或"Sorry for bothering you."）在英语邮件中显得过于谦恭或突兀。

🔧 英语商务邮件/文档的标准结尾模式：
- **需要回复/行动**：Let me know if you have any questions. / Please let me know your thoughts.
- **不需要回复**：Thanks for reading. / Hope this helps.
- **需要审批**：Looking forward to your feedback. / Happy to iterate.
- **异步更新**：No action needed — just keeping you in the loop. / FYI.

而且不要每封邮件都道歉——感谢 > 道歉。

✏️ 改写以下结尾：
1. That's all. Thank you for your time. (你在写周报)
2. Sorry for the long email. (你在写技术方案)

## 3. Sentence-Making Practice — 中译英翻译练习

以下 10 个中文句子涵盖了本周的错误类型——冠词、介词、时态、语序、搭配、句式——每一个都是你往后写英语时容易踩的坑。尝试逐句翻译，特别注意标注了错误类型的部分。翻完后对比参考译文。

1. **我有重要信息要告诉大家——明天正式上线。** (冠词: information 可数吗？有无冠词？)

2. **虽然 CI 通过了，但性能测试没有跑完，所以我不敢合并。** (双重连接: 虽然...但...和因为...所以...只能选一个连词)

3. **当我上个月查监控的时候，数据库已经宕了 20 分钟了。** (过去完成时: 两个过去动作谁先谁后？"已经"怎么表达？)

4. **你能详细说明这台机器是怎么工作的吗？** (嵌入式问句: explain 后面的问句语序？)

5. **我每天 8 点起来开电脑看监控。** (开电脑: open/turn on? 冠词: 监控系统用 the 吗？)

6. **在她负责迁移的那段时间，系统稳定性明显下降了。** (介词: during/at/in? 冠词: stability 泛指还是特指？物主代词 her 还是 the？)

7. **我的经理没有回我邮件——大概太忙了。** (及物动词: reply 后面需要 to 吗？时态: 现在完成还是一般过去？)

8. **你要小心——你刚修的那个模块里面还有很多隐藏的坑。** (关系代词: "你刚修的"是对"模块"的修饰——中间有没有 who/that？)

9. **我真搞不懂为什么大家都不提这事——这个问题我们已经在 retro 里讨论过三次了。** (嵌入式问句: "我真搞不懂为什么..."的语序？时态: 三次讨论用什么时态？)

10. **我觉得还是先扩容比较好——万一不扩，出了事故那就晚了。** (虚拟条件: "万一不扩" = "如果我们不扩容"——Type 2 虚拟？时态怎么后退？)

### 参考译文

1. I have **important information** to share with everyone — we're going live tomorrow.
   - information 不可数 → 零冠词 ∅，不加 a/an
   - "上线/go live"不用 open

2. **Although** the CI passed, the performance tests didn't finish, **so** I'm not comfortable merging.
   - 只用了一個从属连词 although，没有再加 but
   - Alternative: The CI passed, **but** the performance tests didn't finish, **so**... (这里 but 是并列连词，可以)

3. When I checked the monitoring dashboard last month, the database **had already been** down for 20 minutes.
   - 查监控（过去）← 数据库宕机发生在更早 → 用过去完成时
   - "已经"用 already + 过去完成时 had been

4. Could you explain in detail **how this machine works**?
   - 嵌入式问句：主谓不倒装 → how this machine works（不是 how does this machine work）
   - "详细说明" → explain in detail（不是 explain detailedly）

5. I **turn on** my computer every morning at 8 to check the dashboards.
   - 开电脑/电器 → turn on（不是 open）
   - dashboards 泛指 → 零冠词（如果特指某一套系统，用 the）

6. **During** the period when she was responsible for the migration, **the** system's stability noticeably declined.
   - during + 一段时间（不是 at/in）
   - stability 后有 of 短语限定 → the stability
   - her (物主代词，不是 the)

7. My manager **hasn't replied to** my email — probably too busy.
   - reply 不及物 → reply to someone / reply to an email
   - 用现在完成时（从发邮件到现在持续未回复，对"现在"有影响）

8. Be careful — the module **that** you just fixed still has a lot of hidden traps.
   - "你刚修的"修饰"模块" → 需要关系代词 that
   - 正式写作中关系代词最好保留（口语中有时省略宾语从句的 that）

9. I honestly don't understand **why nobody brings this up** — **we've discussed** this issue in the retro three times already.
   - 嵌入式问句：why nobody brings this up（动词在主语后，不倒装）
   - "讨论过三次"对现在有影响 → 现在完成时 have discussed

10. I think we should scale up now — **if we didn't**, and an incident happened, it'd be too late.
    - "万一不扩" = 与现在/未来可能相反（Type 2 虚拟）→ If we didn't (scale up)
    - 主句用 would 表示假设结果

## 4. Weekend Review — 周末回顾与自测

### 4.1 错误诊断练习 — 30 题答案

回顾 1.10 节的 30 个诊断句子，以下是答案与解析：

1. The documentation of the microservices **is** outdated. (主语是 documentation, 单数)
2. We need to discuss ~~about~~ the incident in standup. (discuss 是及物动词)
3. I have ~~a~~ good news to share. (news 不可数 → some good news)
4. When the migration **completes**, the new schema will be active. (时间状语从句用现在时)
5. I don't know what **the best way to test this is**. (嵌入式问句不倒装)
6. **When I looked** at the monitoring dashboard, the memory leak was obvious. (Looking 的主语应该是 I，不是 memory leak)
7. If I **have** bandwidth, I can take on the refactoring task. (Type 1 条件: If + present, will/can)
8. Every engineer on the SRE team **has** access. (Every + 单数动词)
9. She went to the conference room **to attend** the review. (两个动词需要 to 连接表目的)
10. The team completed the sprint, **and** the stakeholder was satisfied. (逗号不能连接两个独立句)
11. Although the fix worked, ~~but~~ the root cause is still unknown. (双重连词)
12. The performance of our APIs **is** critical. (主语是 performance, 单数)
13. I **appreciate your help on this issue very much**. (very much 在句末)
14. **Could you** please review my pull request? (Could you please / Please could you 语序错误)
15. Writing unit tests **is** an investment. (Writing tests 动名词短语作主语 → 单数)
16. Written in Rust, **the new library was found by the team to be** very fast. (分词短语的主语要匹配主句主语)
17. I **understand** the constraints. (understand 是静态动词，不用进行时)
18. The build failed **because** the database migration was incompatible, **and** we rolled back. (because 已经是从属连词，so 多余)
19. He is one of the few developers who **understand** the legacy system. (who 指 developers，复数)
20. **∅ Code quality** is more important than **∅ speed of delivery** to the client. (抽象概念泛指，零冠词)
21. **I think we should prioritize this problem** for the next sprint. (取消话题化结构)
22. I look forward to **hearing** from you. (look forward to + -ing，to 是介词)
23. **Testing is important** for software quality. (去掉 "As we all know")
24. **It's not convenient for me** to join the meeting at that time. (不能说 I am convenient)
25. Neither the PM nor the engineers **were** aware. (neither...nor 就近一致：engineers 是复数)
26. The deadline is approaching, could you please ~~to~~ update your progress. (please + 动词原形)
27. Based on the metrics, **we identified** the performance degradation. (悬垂: Based on 的主语应该是 we)
28. The database **could time out** under peak load. (更简洁: could/might + verb)
29. **Thanks for taking the time to answer this question.** (感谢 > 道歉)
30. The reason is **that** the cache had expired. (the reason is that... 不是 because)

### 4.3 结构性改错 — 综合练习

纠错以下段落——这是一个中国工程师写的周报。它包含了本文讨论的 12+ 个错误。找出尽可能多的错误并修改。

**原始段落:**

> As we all know, last week our team worked on the new authentication module. We discussed about the architecture in Monday's standup. I think the documentation of the microservices are not complete enough. Because we need to launch soon, so we should prioritize this. Every engineers have different opinions about the API design. I don't know what is the best way to proceed. If I will have the time, I would like to set up a meeting discuss the options. The new library which it uses is written in Rust, I think it's very performant. In the end, I want to say that's all. Thank you for your time. Sorry for the long email.

**改后段落** (参考答案):

> Last week, our team worked on the new authentication module. We discussed the architecture at Monday's standup. I think the documentation for the microservices is not complete enough. Because we need to launch soon, we should prioritize this. Every engineer has different opinions about the API design. I don't know what the best way to proceed is. If I have time, I would like to set up a meeting to discuss the options. The new library it uses is written in Rust, and I think it's very performant. Let me know if you have any questions or feedback. Thanks for reading!

**错误清单:**
1. "As we all know" → 删除（修辞 filler）
2. "discussed about" → discussed（及物动词）
3. "in Monday's standup" → at（时间介词: on Monday, at the standup, at Monday's standup）
4. "are not complete" → is（主语是 documentation, 单数）
5. "Because...so" → 删掉 so（双重连词: Because + 从句, 主句）
6. "Every engineers have" → Every engineer has（Every + 单数名词 + 单数动词）
7. "what is the best way" → what the best way...is（嵌入式问句）
8. "If I will have the time" → If I have time（条件句: If + present, will/would）
9. "discuss the options" → to discuss the options（不定式表示目的）
10. "which it uses" → 删除 it 或改为 that it uses → that it uses 或 which it uses (it 多余，which 已经在从句中作宾语)
11. "written in Rust, I think" → written in Rust. I think / written in Rust, and I think（comma splice）
12. "In the end, I want to say that's all" → 删除（中式客套结尾）
13. "Thank you for your time. Sorry for the long email." → Let me know if you have any questions.（正面结尾）

### 4.4 反思与行动计划

本周围绕 52 个常见错误系统工作。现在回头看看：

- 在 1.10 的 30 道诊断题中，你对了多少题？
- 在 52 个错误中，哪些是你最常犯的？（标注 3-5 个"高频错误"）
- 看 4.3 的段落改错——你改出的所有错误吗？有没有漏掉的？

**本周最重要的收获：** 中文和英语之间的差异不是表面现象——它们是两种完全不同的思维系统的体现。当你写英语时，如果脑子里还是中文的结构（语序、冠词缺席、介词系统、连词习惯），你的英语必然带有"中文味"。从现在开始，把你在本周学到的每一个错误都当成一个**主动的检查点**——写完后在脑中逐个过一遍：

- 每个单数可数名词前有 a/an/the 吗？
- 每个及物动词后面有没有多加的介词？
- 每个嵌入式问句是自然语序吗？
- 每个 -s 和 -ed 加了吗？
- 我的状语放在句子末尾还是动词前面？

这些检查点，就是你从"翻译式英语"到"思维式英语"的过渡练习。
